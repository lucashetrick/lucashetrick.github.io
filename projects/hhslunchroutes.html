HHS Running Routes

  1. SeaTac Park

The OG route. This was the first route that I ran during lunch. It’s mostly flat, pretty chill. I like to go out of the front of the school, then run down 152nd until I get to des moines memorial drive, then just run across when clear. There are 2 freeway entrance crossings as well as one stoplight. However, they’re not too bad, and you can easily run them without getting hit (you do need to be pretty cautious, though) Once you get past the stoplight, run straight on the trail to SeaTac Park. You can turn around at 136th street at the turn around point if you want a 3 mile easy run. If you're hauling it, you can run across 136th into SeaTac park and run the cross country loop for an additional 1 mile. You can also turn around at the 144th street light for an easy 2 miles. This route is pretty gray and industrial until you get past 144th street, then it gets better.

  1. SeaTac Muddy Trail

This is a variation on the SeaTac Park route. Instead of going straight at the light at the des moines memorial drive / 144th street intersection, instead take a right. Go down the road for maybe 300 meters, then there will be a bunch of mud. Go down that trail. If you follow the trail you will end up at the pat ryan memorial field. You can run laps there, or explore other trails. I like this route because you get to go in the woods. My only gripes are that sometimes there’s semi trucks on 144th street, and one time there were crazy dirt bikers on the trail, and they almost hit me.

  1. Airport Way

We run this way sometimes during cross-country and track. Get to des moines memorial drive, then take a right, and go left at the light with the sand. There is a nice trail by the side of the road that goes past the airport. This route is nice because there aren’t very many super sketch stoplights to run through. If you want 3 miles, I believe a good turn around point is LSG sky chefs. If you keep going and take a left at the light, you can run to glacier middle school.

  1. Des Moines Memorial Drive

Go the same way as the airport way, but then go straight at the light. If you’re really aware, you can run through it. We also go this way during cross country. It’s pretty chill. Turn around at the “bridge” for a 3 mile run, keep going till the stoplight for longer.

  1. 8th Avenue to Kennedy Catholic Way

I just pioneered this route the other day with my man JP. Run north on 8th ave until you reach 136s street, then hang a left and run till across the bridge. At 4th ave you can see kennedy HS. This route is mediocre. You don’t have to run any stop lights, so it’s probably the safest route in terms of getting hit. However, you do have to run through some sketch neighborhoods, and it's as hilly as hell. I also didn’t like all the construction that was going on

  1. Crazy Route

Only ran this route once. It was pretty dangerous. Crossed 1st ave on the right from 152nd street. Ran south, then up the hill. Ended up at a point that was halfway around the lake Burien loop that I ran from home and for cross country. I like that we ran to Lake Burien, but it was pretty sketchy in my opinion. Not recommended if you’re looking for something safe. Don’t run this route every day.

  1. Sylvester Road

This is my favorite way. Go out the back of the school. Wait for the light at 1st avenue to cross the road. Take the way by Azteca then through Andy's gas station. You;ll pass sylvester ms and then you can keep going to the cove

  1. The Cove

The Cove is the ultimate destination if you’re an HHS student running during lunchtime. It’s not for the faint of heart. According to Google Maps, it’s 2.5 miles to reach the Cove, which makes it about 5 miles round trip. If you want to get there in time, the best approach is to run down Sylvester Road, then take a shortcut down to the beach. I’ve only attempted it twice. The first time was a failure because I took a wrong turn and ended up at a dead end a quarter mile from the beach. I was out of time so I had to turn around. The second time, I was more prepared. I ran during a “mastery day”, our school’s sucky equivalent of finals week, so lunch was 3 minutes longer. I left my backpack in 3rd period as well instead of dropping it off in the library. Finally, I reviewed google maps a lot so I knew exactly where I was going. The cutoff that I missed that I was talking about earlier is SW 176th street. At the dead end there will be a trail that takes you to sw miller street. From there, just go in the direction of the beach and there will be an easily visible trail that takes you there. The run back up that short hill is terrible, but it’s not so back once you get on the main road. Anyways, if you’re gonna attempt this route, you gotta be on your game. I was running at low 7 minute pace the whole time. If you’re not that fast, it’s not recommended.
